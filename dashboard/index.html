Historical patterns — weather-related 311 reports per month

ℹ How to read this heatmap
Grid heatmap for historical review: each row is a district or neighbourhood (follows the Boundary buttons below), each column a month of the selected date range (Quick range / From–To below), cell color = count of weather-related 311 reports that month — flooding/ponding, drainage, snow, windrows, plowing/blading, icy conditions and extreme weather (darker = more; both seasons included so summer and winter patterns can be compared). Pick a district in the filter bar above to zoom the rows to it — or click any row label to apply that district to the map, station table and heatmap at once (click again to clear). Changing the range or boundary also flips the map to 311-density view so all three stay in sync. Hover any cell for the exact count. Note the first/last columns can be partial months (the range may start or end mid-month), so they read lighter than a full month would. Gauge severity has no public history — the rainfall feed is a live snapshot only, so a severity-by-month grid cannot be built from the source (this dashboard never invents history); 311 volume is the closest published month-by-month signal.

Where it is raining hardest
ℹ How to read this map (legend, layers, clicks)
Each ward is filled by its worst gauge, light yellow → dark red: 0 2 3 5 6+ · gray = no gauge (no visibility). Dots are the gauges themselves — readings apply at the gauge point; the ward fill is posture, not certainty. Click a ward for its stations, a dot for that station's 24h rainfall line. Context layers (checkboxes beside the map): P = pump station · ! = road disruption (red = emergency) · 5 = 311 flood reports, last 14 days — the badge number is the exact report count for that neighbourhood (red = underpass flooding; neighbourhood centroid, daily feed) · ▣ = live traffic camera — 58 city intersections, click for live video without driving out. Click a district for its gauges + crew context (pumps, closures, 311 reports, nearest camera).
Fill = reports per 30 days (standardized rate, not the raw count) — a district picked over 2 years otherwise looks "worse" than one picked over 1 week purely from accumulating longer, not from being denser; dividing by the span you chose keeps colors comparable across any Quick range/custom dates. Continuous light → dark (not fixed bands) — read the rate off the legend above or a click popup, which also shows the raw count. Counts are weather-related 311 categories (flooding/ponding, drainage, snow, windrows, plowing/blading, icy conditions, extreme weather — both seasons) — a broader set than the 14-day layer's markers, which track only the current mode's flood or snow/ice incident types. Use Quick range for one-click windows or From/To for anything else — Edmonton's 311 open dataset only goes back to Jan 2023 (~3.5 years), so the date pickers won't go earlier than that or past today; a 10-year window is not available from the source. Reports are geocoded to the neighbourhood centroid (daily feed), so this reflects where residents call in most often, not where today's storm is worst — check the severity view above for that. A large share of 311 requests carry no neighbourhood tag (city-wide or unaddressed service types) and can't be placed on this map — the fill shows only the geolocatable subset, not the full request volume. Click a ward/neighbourhood for its exact count.
